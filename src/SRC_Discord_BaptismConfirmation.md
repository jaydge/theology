### 7/4/26, 2:23 AM — Athanasius325 / Fr James

Hey, all! Please keep safe outside; the heat so far has been Florida-level. Here are my responses to the questions: 


1) How do Anglicans understand the relationship between baptism and confirmation with regard to the Holy Spirit? 

2) Do Anglicans believe that baptized Christians already possess the Holy Spirit and His gifts prior to confirmation, or is confirmation understood as conveying something new or additional?

3) Do Anglicans believe that possession of the Holy Spirit happens upon baptism? Meaning, as in Pentecost, the Holy Spirit descends & indwells in the person.


Answer: Baptism is the receiving of the Holy Ghost (Acts 2:38), whereas in Confirmation we receive a "stirring up" or "filling" of the Holy Ghost. The Apostles did receive the Holy Ghost in John 20:21-22, but they also received the Holy Ghost in a deeper way in Acts 2:1-4. We see a similar thing in comparing the promise in Acts 2:38 to Acts 8:14-17. St Paul speaks of this concept of "stirring up" the Holy Ghost in 2 Timothy 1:6-7.

Concerning the stirring up of the Holy Ghost, we see it happening in Acts 8 exclusively by the Apostles being brought to lay hands on the Samaritans (it should be noted that a reason this is happening in Acts 8, especially with the external signs, is that God is emphasizing the universality of the Gospel and Descent of the Holy Ghost successively, as ripples from a tossed stone; Samaritans are not quite Jews but not quite Gentiles, and then with Cornelius we see full-on Gentiles receiving the Gospel). 

That stirring up can be manifested in a variety of ways: a rekindling of the desire to live a life dedicated to God, a recognition of one's vocation, a desire to join or even start some particular ministry or service, etc. 

Concerning a particular age, it is typically 8-12. The expectation if that the child knows: the Apostles' Creed, the Decalogue, the Two Great Commandments, the Lord's Prayer, and some basics concerning the two Dominical Sacraments.
In the 1928 Prayer Book, there are two Offices of Instruction (pg283-295) and a Catechism (pg 577-583); these help with understanding some of the basics of the Faith for children.
